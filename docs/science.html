What's happening in classrooms.

Teachers are burning out while students fall behind — and the current wave of AI tools is making both worse.

25%

Math proficiency at a 20-year low

About 1 in 4 U.S. 8th graders score proficient in math (NAEP). Speed isn't the missing ingredient — depth is.

53%

Teachers report severe burnout

Educators are asked to personalize instruction for every student, without tools built for it.

Showing up isn't the same as learning.

For the last decade, EdTech has optimized for one thing: getting students to the right answer as fast as possible. By acting as answer engines rather than reasoning partners, these tools encourage students to offload their thinking.

The result:

Students show up, but check out. When the answer is always one tap away, the friction that makes learning stick disappears.
